Count to Ten Meditation

May 28, 2020

This is a basic meditation technique that I practice.

  1. Sit comfortably, either cross legged or in a chair, and with an upright posture. Imagine a string going out the top of your head like a puppet and pulling gently on the top of your spine.
  2. Close your eyes (or if you prefer keep them open) and take a few deep breaths to get settled.
  3. Once you are settled, breath in slowly through your nose and then out blissfully, focusing on the sensation of the air going in and out of your nostrils.
  4. After each exhalation count silently to yourself 1,2,3, etc... until you reach the count of 10.
  5. Once you reach 10 simply begin again at 1.
  6. If you become distracted by thoughts, sounds, or sensations, and forget what number you are on, it's OK and completely natural. Simply bring your attention back to the breath and start again
  7. Repeat as long as suits you, 5 minutes, 10 minutes, 1 hour, it does not matter where, when, or how long. Remember, the point is not to count to 10, but rather to work with your mind's attention and the thoughts and sensations that are ever-present.
