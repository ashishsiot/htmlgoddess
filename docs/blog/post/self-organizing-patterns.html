Patterns of Self Organization

May 29, 2020

Something that I'm trying to achieve with this framework is to make the structure of things self organizing so that an increase in the complexity of the elements and content is organized naturally and does not require more complex patterns or knowledge to handle. This is something I've noticed with many frameworks. As the framework gets applied to more and more use cases, more and more complex additions get added, which requires yet more knowledge to maintain properly. This addition in complexity cost inevitably means that these frameworks will not be applied properly, and can result in costly maintenance and issues. words, bloat. Perhaps this is an innate aspect of what happens design patterns when they get applied in the real world, but nevertheless I hope to mitigate that effect as much as possible.

What do I mean by "self organizing"? In nature, there exist certain patterns that have allowed organisms and ecosystems to develop to an arbitrary complexity based on a few simple rules. One such example is trees which sprout branches at a specific ratio. [ratio here] Furthermore, the trees themselves have been found to be sized and spaced around each other at this specific ratio. The same pattern is found within our circulatory system in the human body, the spirals of sea shells, and galaxies. One can wonder why, but my theory is that evolution has selected this pattern again and again because it works. I've always found the best frameworks to have this fractal aspect to it, that makes it so that you can think at a "relative" level, and not have to worry about the entire system as a whole.

This is the reason why I've opted to include some of these constraints in this framework. By avoiding classes, it limits in how much you can customize but also preempts the pitfalls of arbitrary styles and classes. You can now take any stylesheet from anywhere in the world that targets plain HTML and just drop it in.

By avoiding javascript, you lose the ability to manipulate content dynamically, but you gain the speed and ease of use that comes with simplified state management.

With using html tags for the templating, you require the user to only know one language.

Using the filesystem for the url routing also allows the user to think naturally about how they want to structure their content. It's intuitive to think that how you access your pages in a browsers will work the same as how you access your files on your computer. It's true that at a certain point you have two much content and then it can become cumbersome but I believe for the majority of people making websites this is actually not an issue.
